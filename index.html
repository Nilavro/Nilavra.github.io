About

I work at the intersection of machine learning, economics, and control, with a focus on programmatic decision-making systems for marketing, bidding, budget allocation, measurement, and counterfactual evaluation. My research interests include reinforcement learning, system identification, regret-aware policy evaluation, and simulation-based decision environments.

Research Themes

Control for Marketing Systems

Feedback control, response-curve forecasting, ROAS target steering, and automated budget/spend decision systems.

Regret-Aware Evaluation

Retrospective regret, model-based oracle benchmarks, constrained counterfactuals, and uncertainty-aware allocation auditing.

Simulation and Digital Twins

Semi-synthetic environments for evaluating bidding, allocation, energy, and smart-city decision policies under realistic dynamics.

Selected Projects

  • Response Curve Forecasting and Budget Optimization. Production-scale modeling and control systems for marketing spend allocation and automated decision support.
  • Certified Retrospective Regret for Allocation Policies. Framework for measuring dollars left on the table using constraint-faithful hindsight oracles.
  • Nonparametric Marginality Zone Detection. Distributional framework for identifying operating regions where marginal gains become locally unresolved or economically indifferent.
  • Energy Economist / DataCenterGym Extensions. Semi-synthetic evaluation environments for data-center, grid, smart-city, and economic impact questions.
