This research direction extends decision-environment thinking to energy, infrastructure, and smart-city systems.

The goal is to build semi-synthetic simulation environments that help evaluate the economic and operational impact of data centers, power-grid constraints, energy demand, urban infrastructure, and policy interventions.

Key themes include:

- Data-center energy demand modeling.
- Regional elasticity forecasting.
- Smart-city decision environments.
- Semi-synthetic benchmark construction.
- Economic evaluation of cyber-physical systems.
- Digital twins for infrastructure planning.
