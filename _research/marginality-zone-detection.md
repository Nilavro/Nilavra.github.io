title: "Nonparametric Marginality Zone Detection"
summary: "Distributional methods for identifying operationally meaningful marginality regions."
---

This project studies how to identify spend regions where marginal gains become locally ambiguous, weak, or economically indifferent.

Rather than relying only on a global parametric response curve, the framework compares local spend and profit distributions across neighboring regions. The goal is to detect operational zones where spend changes no longer produce reliably directional evidence.

Key themes include:

- Distributional comparison of neighboring spend regions.
- Stochastic dominance and probability-of-superiority tests.
- Regime-change detection.
- Coarse-to-refined zone construction.
- Operational decision regions under uncertainty.
