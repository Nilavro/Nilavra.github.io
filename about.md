I am **Nilavra Pathak**, a **Senior Machine Learning Scientist at Expedia Group**, where I work on large-scale machine learning and decision systems for marketing science.

My work sits at the intersection of **machine learning, economics, system identification, and control**. I am interested in how we can replace heuristic, manually tuned business decision-making with systematic, measurable, and feedback-driven decision systems. In practice, this includes problems such as marketing budget allocation, bidding, campaign automation, response-curve forecasting, counterfactual measurement, and regret-aware policy evaluation.

A major theme in my work is building systems that do not only predict outcomes, but also support better decisions under uncertainty. This includes estimating how spend affects returns, designing control mechanisms for steering business targets, constructing model-based benchmarks for historical decisions, and evaluating whether alternative allocation policies could have produced better outcomes under realistic operational constraints.

My current research interests include:

- **Response-curve modeling and system identification** for marketing and allocation systems.
- **Control and reinforcement learning** for sequential decision-making under business constraints.
- **Retrospective regret analysis** for auditing historical allocation decisions.
- **Counterfactual evaluation and measurement** when live experimentation is limited or expensive.
- **Nonparametric marginality detection** for identifying operationally stable spend regions.
- **Semi-synthetic simulation and digital twins** for testing decision policies before deployment.
- **Economic evaluation of cyber-physical systems**, including energy, data centers, smart cities, and infrastructure planning.

Before joining Expedia Group, my doctoral research focused on **energy and building analytics**, using probabilistic machine learning and system identification to model building systems. That background continues to shape my research agenda: I am interested in decision environments where machine learning, control, and economics come together to improve real-world systems.

Across both industry and research, my goal is to build reliable decision frameworks that make complex allocation problems more transparent, measurable, and operationally useful.
