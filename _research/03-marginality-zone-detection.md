This project studies how to identify spend regions where marginal gains become locally ambiguous, weak, or economically indifferent.

Rather than relying only on a global parametric response curve, the framework compares local spend and profit distributions across neighboring regions.

Key themes include:

- Distributional comparison of neighboring spend regions.
- Stochastic dominance and probability-of-superiority tests.
- Regime-change detection.
- Coarse-to-refined zone construction.
- Operational decision regions under uncertainty.
